Section 01 // Core Concept

Genes, survival & time

Living things pass on genes — instructions written in DNA — to their offspring. Because individuals vary, some survive better than others, and their genes spread. Over many generations a whole species can change. That change is evolution.

Inheritance

Offspring resemble their parents because they inherit copies of their genes. Genes sit on chromosomes, which are made of DNA.

Alleles

Different versions of a gene are called alleles. A dominant allele shows even if only one copy is present; a recessive one needs two copies to show.

Natural selection

Variation + a struggle to survive = the best-suited individuals live, reproduce, and pass on their helpful genes. Darwin called this survival of the fittest.

Evolution

Repeat natural selection over thousands of generations and a species gradually changes — sometimes into an entirely new species.

Section 04 // Encrypted: GCSE Stretch

Advanced data

🔒 DNA, genotype & the evidence
DNA is a twisted ladder — a double helix. A short section that codes for one feature is a gene.
Genotype = the alleles you carry (e.g. Rr). Phenotype = the feature you actually show (can roll). Homozygous = two of the same (RR or rr); heterozygous = two different (Rr).
Selective breeding: humans choosing which animals or plants reproduce — like natural selection, but we pick the survivors (e.g. dogs, crops).
Evidence for evolution: fossils show how life changed over millions of years; antibiotic-resistant bacteria show natural selection happening fast, right now.

Extinction happens when a species cannot adapt fast enough to a change — no individuals survive to reproduce.
