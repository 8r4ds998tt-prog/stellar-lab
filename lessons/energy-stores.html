Section 01 // Core Concept

Stores & pathways

Energy is held in stores and moved between them along pathways. The golden rule — the law of conservation of energy — says the total never changes: energy can't be created or destroyed, only transferred.

Energy stores

Where energy sits, waiting.

Energy pathways

The four ways energy moves between stores.

Conservation

Add up all the energy before and after a transfer and you get the same total. It just ends up in different stores.

Dissipation

In every real transfer some energy spreads out to the surroundings — usually as heat. This energy isn't destroyed, but it's no longer useful. We call it wasted.

Section 03 // Waste Not

Cutting the waste

Engineers try to reduce wasted energy so more goes where it's wanted. The less you waste, the more efficient the machine.

🛢️

Lubrication

Oil between moving parts reduces friction, so less energy is wasted as heat and sound.

🧥

Insulation

Loft insulation and double glazing slow heat escaping, keeping thermal energy where it's useful.

💡

Better tech

An LED bulb sends most energy to light; an old filament bulb wasted ~90% as heat.

🔄

Streamlining

Smooth shapes cut air resistance (drag), so vehicles waste less energy pushing through air.

Section 04 // Encrypted: GCSE Stretch

Advanced data

🔒 Efficiency, power & calculations
Efficiency = useful energy out ÷ total energy in × 100%. A motor that turns 75 J of 100 J into movement is 75% efficient.
Power is how fast energy is transferred: power = energy ÷ time, measured in watts (W). 1 W = 1 joule per second.
Kinetic energy: KE = ½ × m × v². Gravitational store: GPE = m × g × h (g ≈ 9.8 N/kg).

Sankey diagrams show energy flow as arrows whose width is the amount of energy — a wide useful arrow and a thin wasted arrow means a more efficient device.
