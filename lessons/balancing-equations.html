Atoms are never created or destroyed in a reaction — only rearranged. Balance the equations so both sides match.

Section 01 // Core Concept

Conservation of mass

Atoms in = Atoms out

Every atom on the left (reactants) must appear on the right (products). None vanish, none appear from nowhere.

Total mass stays the same

Because no atoms are lost, the total mass before a reaction equals the total mass after it.

  • Count each type of atom on both sides.
  • To fix a shortfall, put a big number (coefficient) in front of a formula — it multiplies the whole formula.
  • Never change the small numbers (subscripts) inside a formula — that would change the chemical itself.
  • Keep adjusting until every element matches.

🔒 Method, state symbols & tips

A reliable method: balance metals first, then non-metals, then hydrogen, then oxygen last (it often appears in several places).

Coefficients multiply everything in the formula. 2H₂O means 2 × (2 H + 1 O) = 4 H and 2 O.

Use the lowest whole numbers. If every coefficient could be halved, halve them — 2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O, not 4, 2, 4.

State symbols (Higher tier) describe each substance: (s) solid, (l) liquid, (g) gas, (aq) dissolved in water.
