Section 01 // Core Concept

The unit of life

Cells are the smallest building blocks of living things. Some organisms are a single cell; you are made of trillions. Animal and plant cells share key parts — and plant cells have a few extras.

Shared parts

Nucleus, cytoplasm, cell membrane, mitochondria and ribosomes are found in both animal and plant cells.

Plant-only extras

Plant cells also have a cell wall, chloroplasts and a permanent vacuole.

Section 03 // Specialised Cells

Built for the job

Cells change shape to do specific jobs — their structure fits their function.

🩸

Red blood cell

No nucleus and a dimpled disc shape — maximum room to carry oxygen.

⚡

Nerve cell

Long and thin to carry electrical signals quickly around the body.

🌾

Root hair cell

A long projection gives a big surface area to absorb water and minerals.

🏊

Sperm cell

A tail (flagellum) and lots of mitochondria to swim to the egg.

Section 04 // Encrypted: GCSE Stretch

Advanced data

🔒 Cell types, microscopes & size
Eukaryotic cells (animals, plants) keep their DNA inside a nucleus.
Prokaryotic cells (bacteria) are much smaller, have no nucleus — their DNA floats free as a loop, plus small rings called plasmids.

Microscopes let us see cells. A light microscope shows cells and a nucleus; an electron microscope reveals tiny detail like ribosomes.

Magnification: magnification = image size ÷ real size. Sizes use micrometres (µm): 1 mm = 1000 µm.
