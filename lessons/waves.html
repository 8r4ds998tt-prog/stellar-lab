Section 01 // Core Concept

Energy on the move

A wave transfers energy from one place to another without transferring matter. The water in a ripple bobs up and down — it doesn't travel across the pond. Waves come in two types.

Transverse waves

The wave moves one way; the wobble is at 90° to it (up–down). Light, water ripples and all electromagnetic waves are transverse.

Longitudinal waves

The wobble is along the same direction the wave travels — squashes and stretches. Sound is longitudinal.

The four measurements

Amplitude (size of the wobble), wavelength (length of one wave), frequency (waves per second, in hertz) and wave speed.

The wave equation

wave speed = frequency × wavelength  (v = f × λ). Bigger frequency or longer wavelength means a faster wave.

Section 03 // Sound & Light

Two famous waves

Sound and light are both waves, but they behave very differently.

🔊

Sound is longitudinal

Made by vibrations; it needs a medium (air, water, solids) — there's no sound in space. Higher frequency = higher pitch; bigger amplitude = louder.

💡

Light is transverse

An electromagnetic wave that travels in straight lines and needs no medium — it crosses empty space, which is how sunlight reaches us.

⚡

Light wins the race

Light travels about 300,000,000 m/s — a million times faster than sound (~340 m/s). That's why you see lightning before you hear thunder.

🪞

Reflect & bend

Waves reflect off surfaces (echoes, mirrors) and refract (bend) when they pass into a new material — like a straw looking bent in water.

Section 04 // Encrypted: GCSE Stretch

Advanced data

🔒 The wave equation & the EM spectrum
Period & frequency: the period (T) is the time for one wave, in seconds. f = 1 ÷ T — a 2 Hz wave has a period of 0.5 s.
Using the equation: rearrange v = f × λ to find any one — e.g. λ = v ÷ f. Always check units: speed in m/s, frequency in Hz, wavelength in m.
The electromagnetic spectrum is a family of transverse waves, all travelling at the speed of light: radio · microwave · infrared · visible light · ultraviolet · X-ray · gamma. Going that way, wavelength gets shorter and frequency (and energy) gets higher.

Uses of waves: radio & microwaves for communication, infrared for heating and remotes, X-rays for medical imaging, ultrasound for scans and depth-finding.
